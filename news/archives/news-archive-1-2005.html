Survivors of the Megapocalypse & Destiny Deoxys releases
The Survivors of the Megapocalypse Duel Masters TCG expansion was released yesterday, and this month's D-MAX packets were sent out earlier this week with a Megapocalypse set poster, Billion-Degree Dragon art poster, Giriel, Ghastly Warrior (Super Warriors #S3) magnet, Gargako Dragon paper model, and the usual assortment of stickers and ads. Also, the letter mentions that starting this year, all D-MAX members will recieve exclusive awards for attending a certain number of tournaments: a Duel Masters promo card for every 3, and a unique collectors item for every 15 events. The items will be included in the next D-MAX mailing (which is DCI-number tracked). The Super Rares, Very Rares, and Light civilization cards from Megapocalypse are now up on their set's database page (see the New & Updated Pages section on the right nav bar, or the Megapocalypse link on the Duel Masters menu).

I don't know why I haven't posted this yet (I've known about it for weeks), but "Pokémon: Destiny Deoxys", the seventh Pokémon movie, will air on Kids' WB! next Saturday, January 22, at 10am Eastern/9am Pacific. The DVD/VHS release will be on February 15.

The 2005 Pokémon TCG World Championships will be held from August 19 to August 21 at the Town and Country Resort and Convention Center, San Diego, California. The top players in North America from each of the three age groups (10-under, 11-14, and 15-up) from the Pokémon TCG Gym Challenge and Regional Championships series, and the National Championships all get automatic invites. Internationally, each age group's best two from the National Championships and top players from the Stadium Challenges will be invited. Everyone else has a chance to qualify in the "Last Chance Qualifier" tournament on August 19 at the WC2005 venue. The top 32 finishers at the Worlds in each age group will recieve Pokémon prize packs; the top 16 recieve scholarship awards valued between $1,000 an $7,500; and the top three finishers will be awarded a Pokémon TCG Worlds trophy and an automatic invite to the 2006 Championships.

The Pokémon Center New York will be closing temporarily for remodeling, but will reopen this spring with an added showcase of other exclusive Nintendo products.

As far as this site goes, this week's Cards of the Week are up: Professor Oak's Research and King Tsunami. Next week's will be up Wednesday to give these ones their share of the spotlight, and then the ones for the following week will be up on that Monday, as scheduled. The Pokémon Release Dates and Pokémon Emerald pages are up as well.

Survivors of the Megapocalypse prerelese info & tournaments
New info has been released about the upcoming Survivors of the Megapocalypse DM set. The big news about it is that it introduces three new concepts to the game: Speed Attacker, which lets a creature attack the turn it comes into play (a direct adaptation of Haste, for you Magic g33ks); Triple Breaker, which is exactly what it sounds like; and Survivors. Each civ gets 10 creatures and 2 spells, and from what it looks like this set is really going to start making the game interesting (with a lot of twists from Magic...).

Survivors of the Megapocalypse prerelease tournaments will be held this Saturday and Sunday, January 15 and 16, at wherever JDC events are held (check http://www.duelmasters.com/ for locations). The tournaments will be modified Swiss-style sealed-deck (each participant will recieve five Megapocalypse booster packs and will build their deck from that); the first 32 duelists to register get Olgate, Nightmare Samurai (see below) and the top 4 finishers get...more Megapocalypse boosters.

The January Duel Masters JDC prize cards have been announced: Olgate, Nightmare Samurai for the first 32 to register at each event (this card isn't available in a set; 7-for-6000 double breaker demon command; untap whenever one of your creatures is destroyed), and Ballus, Dogfight Enforcer Q from Megapocalypse (5-for-3000; untap whenever your turn ends) for the top 8 finishers.

Pokémon: #46 of The Essential 50
Pokémon has been listed as number 46 on The Essential 50 video games list by 1UP.com, a list of video games that revolutionized the world of video gaming. The Pokémon series is named as the game that changed the weak reputation of handheld gaming as a novelty, and was the first "Game Boy game that used the system's unique linking ability to promote trading and collecting items within the game." (Also among The Essential 50 are the original Super Mario Kart, Myst, Half Life, Doom, Tetris, and Pong. The full list can be viewed at http://www.1up.com/do/feature?cId=3116290.)
